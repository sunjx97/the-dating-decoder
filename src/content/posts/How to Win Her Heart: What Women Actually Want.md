Let’s be real: most advice about “how to get the girl” is written by men who’ve never actually talked to a woman. It’s full of manipulation, mind games, and lines that make us roll our eyes so hard they might get stuck.

I’m Jadwiga, and I’ve been a woman my whole life. I’ve also spent years studying relationship psychology and talking to hundreds of women about what they *actually* want. Spoiler: it’s not a guy who memorized pickup artist scripts.

So if you’re tired of the noise and want the honest truth, keep reading. This is what women wish you knew.

---

## The Foundation: Safety, Not Schemes

Before any spark can happen, a woman needs to feel **safe** with you. Not just physically safe—though that matters too—but emotionally safe. Safe to be herself. Safe to say no. Safe to take things slow without being pressured.

If she feels like you’re performing, running a script, or trying to “win” her, her guard goes up instantly. We can tell. We’ve been dealing with this since we were teenagers.

**The fix:** Be genuine. Be present. Don’t treat her like a conquest. Treat her like a human being you’re genuinely curious about.

---

## What Actually Works (From a Woman’s Mouth)

### 1. Listen Like You Actually Care

Not just waiting for your turn to talk. Not just nodding while you think about what to say next. Real listening.

When she mentions something small—her favorite band, a stressful thing at work, a dream she’s hesitant to share—**remember it.** Bring it up later. Ask how that work thing went. Send her a song by that band.

That’s not “technique.” That’s just paying attention. And it’s ridiculously attractive.

### 2. Be Consistent, Not Hot-and-Cold

Nothing confuses a woman more than a man who’s all in one day and distant the next. If you like her, **show up consistently.** Text back within a reasonable time. Make plans and keep them. Don’t disappear for three days to seem “mysterious.”

Mysterious is just another word for unavailable. And unavailable gets old fast.

### 3. Show, Don’t Just Tell

Anyone can say “I’m a nice guy.” What matters is what you *do*.

- You say you’re interested? **Ask her out.**
- You say you care about her? **Remember the little things.**
- You say you’re different from other guys? **Prove it through your actions over time.**

Women are wired to notice inconsistency. We’ve learned to protect ourselves. So if your words and actions don’t match, we’ll notice—and we’ll pull back.

### 4. Respect Her Autonomy

Nothing is less attractive than a man who tries to control or “manage” a woman. She’s not a project. She’s a whole person with her own life, opinions, and decisions.

- If she says she’s busy, believe her.
- If she has boundaries, respect them.
- If she wants to take things slow, don’t push.

The fastest way to lose her is to make her feel like her voice doesn’t matter.

### 5. Be Vulnerable (Yes, Really)

We don’t want a perfect man. We want a real one. Someone who can admit when he’s scared, when he’s unsure, when he’s hurt. That’s not weakness—that’s trust.

When you open up, it gives her permission to open up too. And that’s where real connection lives.

**Note:** There’s a difference between vulnerability and trauma-dumping on a first date. Read the room. But over time, let her in.

---

## What to Avoid at All Costs

### ❌ Pickup lines and scripted game
We’ve heard them all. They make us feel like a target, not a person.

### ❌ Pretending to like things she likes
If you hate rom-coms, don’t pretend you love them. It’s obvious when you’re faking. Be honest—she’ll appreciate your authenticity more than a shared interest.

### ❌ Love-bombing then ghosting
Coming on super strong then disappearing is emotional whiplash. It’s also a huge red flag. Healthy interest is steady, not explosive.

### ❌ Trying to “win” an argument just to be right
Sometimes we just want to be heard, not debated. If she’s sharing a feeling, don’t tell her why she’s wrong to feel it. Just listen.

---

## The Truth About “Nice Guys”

A quick note: being kind is great. But “nice” isn’t a transaction. If you’re only nice because you expect something in return (sex, a relationship, her attention), that’s not genuine kindness—it’s manipulation.

Real nice guys are just… nice. They don’t keep score. And ironically, that’s what makes them attractive.

---

## One Final Thought

The woman you want isn’t looking for a checklist of traits. She’s looking for someone who makes her feel **seen, safe, and valued.** Someone who laughs with her, not at her. Someone who’s curious about who she is, not just what she looks like.

Be that guy. Not for her—for yourself. Because that’s the kind of person worth being.
