You spend hours picking the right photos, writing a bio, and swiping. But the matches are few, and the conversations go nowhere. Sound familiar?

Here’s the truth: most dating profiles are boring. They all say the same things: “I love to travel,” “I’m looking for a partner in crime,” “Just ask.” If you want to stand out, you need to show who you *actually* are—not who you think people want you to be.

Let’s break down how to write a profile that works.

---

## 1. The Photos: Show, Don’t Just Tell

Your photos are the first thing people see. They should give a sense of your life, your personality, and what it might be like to hang out with you.

### ✅ Do:
- **One clear face shot** – Smiling, looking at the camera, no sunglasses.
- **One full-body shot** – Be honest about your body type.
- **One action shot** – Doing something you love: hiking, cooking, playing music.
- **One social shot** – With friends (but make it clear which one is you).
- **One candid or funny shot** – Shows your personality.

### ❌ Don’t:
- Use only group photos (too confusing).
- Use old photos (deceiving).
- Use photos with exes cropped out (we can tell).
- Lead with a bathroom selfie (please don’t).

**Pro tip:** If you’re a guy, studies show photos with a dog increase matches. Just make sure it’s actually your dog.

---

## 2. The Bio: Be Specific, Not Generic

The biggest mistake people make is writing a bio that could apply to anyone. “I like going out and staying in.” “I’m easygoing.” “I love adventure.” These words mean nothing.

Instead, get specific.

| Generic | Better |
|--------|--------|
| “I love to travel.” | “My passport has stamps from 12 countries. Next stop: Japan.” |
| “I’m a foodie.” | “I’ll try anything once—except durian. That stuff is scary.” |
| “I enjoy hiking.” | “I’ve hiked all over the Alps. Will you keep up?” |
| “I’m looking for a partner in crime.” | “Looking for someone to try that new taco place with and then argue about who makes better guacamole.” |

See the difference? Specific details spark conversation. They give the other person something to reply to.

---

## 3. The “About Me” Section: Tell a Mini-Story

Instead of listing traits, tell a quick story about your life right now.

**Example for a woman:**  
“I’m a graphic designer who spends too much money on iced lattes and vintage dresses. On weekends you’ll find me at flea markets, in a yoga class, or trying to convince my friends to watch another episode of *The Office* for the tenth time.”

**Example for a man:**  
“I teach high school history by day, play guitar in a cover band by night. I’m probably the only person you’ll meet who can name all the US presidents in order *and* quote every line from *The Big Lebowski*.”

These bios paint a picture. They make you sound like a real person, not a dating app clone.

---

## 4. What You’re Looking For: Be Honest

It’s okay to be clear about what you want—just don’t be demanding or negative.

❌ “No drama, no hookups, no liars.” (Negative)
✅ “Looking for a genuine connection that could lead to something real.” (Positive)

❌ “Swipe left if you’re not fit.” (Rude)
✅ “I’m into active lifestyles—would love someone to hike with.” (Inviting)

**Pro tip:** If you’re open to casual dating, say “I’m enjoying meeting new people and seeing where things go.” If you want a relationship, say “I’d love to find someone to build something meaningful with.” Clarity attracts the right people and filters out the wrong ones.

---

## 5. The Opener: Give Them a Hook

The best profiles end with a question or an invitation. It makes it easy for someone to message you.

- “If you could travel anywhere tomorrow, where would you go?”
- “What’s the best meal you’ve had recently?”
- “Tell me your favorite bad joke.”
- “Be warned: I will challenge you to a board game and I don’t lose easily.”

When someone has something to respond to, they’re much more likely to message.

---

## 6. A Few Final Don’ts

- **Don’t write a novel.** Keep it to a few short paragraphs. Leave some mystery.
- **Don’t list dealbreakers.** It comes off as negative. Save that for later.
- **Don’t be too serious.** Humor goes a long way.
- **Don’t lie.** It always comes out eventually.

---

## A Note for Women and Men

**For women:** You might get a lot of messages anyway. A good profile helps you filter for quality. Be specific about what you want—it scares off the wrong guys and attracts the right ones.

**For men:** You’re competing for attention. A great profile is your best tool. Put in the effort. It pays off.

---

## Your Profile, Your First Impression

Think of your profile as a first date before the first date. It’s your chance to show who you are and attract someone who actually likes that person.

Take an hour to rewrite yours. Ask a friend to read it. Test different versions. And remember: the goal isn’t to appeal to everyone—it’s to appeal to the right one.
