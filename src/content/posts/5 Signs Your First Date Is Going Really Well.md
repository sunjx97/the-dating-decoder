You’ve showered, you’ve stressed over your outfit, and you’ve made it through the first twenty minutes of awkward small talk. Now you’re sitting across from your date, wondering: *Is this going anywhere? Are they into me? Should I order another drink or start planning my escape?*

We’ve all been there. The good news? Most people give off pretty clear signals when they’re enjoying themselves—you just need to know what to look for. Here are five surefire signs your first date is going well, plus one sneaky red flag to keep in mind.

## 1. The Conversation Flows Without Effort

When a date is going well, you won’t be fighting for things to say. Awkward silences are replaced by natural pauses where you’re both just smiling at each other, or someone jumps in with “Oh, that reminds me of…”

If you find yourselves finishing each other’s sentences, laughing at inside jokes that formed twenty minutes ago, or accidentally talking for two hours without checking your phone—that’s chemistry. Real connection makes time fly.

**What it sounds like:**  
- “Wait, you also backpacked through Thailand? That’s insane!”  
- “No way, I thought I was the only one who still watches that show!”  
- (Laughter, lots of it.)

## 2. They’re Leaning In (Literally)

Body language doesn’t lie. If your date is leaning toward you across the table, angling their body your way, or propping their chin on their hand while you talk, they’re engaged. They want to be closer.

Watch for subtle touches, too. A hand briefly grazing yours while reaching for a glass, a playful tap on your arm after a joke—these small gestures mean they’re comfortable and looking for an excuse to make contact.

**Pro tip:** If they mirror your movements (you lean in, they lean in; you pick up your drink, they do the same), that’s subconscious rapport. It’s a golden sign.

## 3. They Ask Personal Questions (and Actually Listen)

Small talk is safe: “What do you do?” “Where do you live?” When someone is genuinely interested, they’ll dig deeper. They’ll ask about your childhood, your passions, your weirdest travel story. And when you answer, they’ll follow up with more questions, remembering details you mentioned earlier.

This shows they’re not just waiting for their turn to speak—they’re trying to understand who you are.

**Red flag alert:** If the conversation feels like an interview where only one person is asking questions, that’s a bad sign. A good date is a two-way street.

## 4. They’re Not Glancing at Their Phone

In 2026, this is a big one. If your date keeps their phone on the table, face down, and doesn’t sneak peeks at notifications, they’re present. If they actually *turn it off* or leave it in their pocket? They’re a keeper.

On the flip side, if they check their phone more than once—especially during a lull—they’re either bored or anxious to be elsewhere. Trust that feeling.

## 5. They Bring Up Future Plans

When someone says, “We should totally try that new taco place next time,” or “Oh, you’d love my friend’s band—maybe we could catch a show together,” they’re already imagining a second date. It’s an unconscious way of saying, *I want to see you again.*

Even a vague “This was fun, we should do it again sometime” counts—as long as it sounds genuine, not like a polite goodbye script.

## The One Red Flag That Overrules Everything

You can have all five signs going for you, but if something feels *off*—a knot in your stomach, a sense that you’re performing rather than being yourself—pay attention. Sometimes two people can have a “perfect” date on paper but zero romantic spark. And that’s okay.

The goal isn’t to check every box; it’s to walk away feeling excited, curious, and eager to see them again. Trust your gut. It knows.
